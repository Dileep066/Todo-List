The Silent Language of Dining

Understanding the unspoken rules of etiquette.

Posture Matters

Sit upright, but not stiffly. Good posture signals confidence and engagement. Avoid slouching over your plate.

Eye Contact

Maintain a gentle, confident gaze to show you are listening and build rapport with your companions.

Gestures

Use subtle, controlled hand movements to animate your conversation. Avoid pointing with your utensils.

Facial Expressions

A pleasant, open expression makes you appear friendly. Smile genuinely and react appropriately.

Hands on the Table

In many cultures, it's best to keep hands visible. Rest your wrists on the table edge when not eating.

Personal Space

Be mindful of others' space. Don't crowd your neighbor or intrude on their "table territory."

Hygiene & Health Etiquette

Wash Hands

Always wash your hands before and after meals to ensure cleanliness.

Avoid Touching Face

Refrain from touching your face or hair while at the dining table.

Cover Coughs

Properly cover all coughs and sneezes, preferably into your elbow.

Don't Share Utensils

Avoid sharing utensils or double-dipping to maintain hygiene for everyone.

Keep Items Off Table

Personal belongings like phones and keys should not be on the dining table.

Clean Up After Yourself

Leave your dining area as neat as you found it, showing respect for the space.
